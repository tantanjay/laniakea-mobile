# Laniakea — Product Roadmap (Private Reflection System)

## Core Philosophy

Laniakea is not an AI therapist, and it is not a diagnostic system.

It is a **privacy-first reflection environment** where:
- All computation happens on-device
- All insights come from the user's own writing
- The system observes language patterns, not emotions or mental states

The goal is:

> Help users understand how their writing changes over time  
> without interpreting or judging their psychology

---

## Core Differentiator

**Private reflection with local intelligence.**

Unlike cloud AI journaling apps, Laniakea is built around:
- On-device embeddings
- No external data transmission
- User-owned meaning
- Structural language analysis instead of emotional labeling

---

## What Already Exists

### Current Features
- Journal entry system
- Calendar-based navigation
- Vibe Gauge (Joy ↔ Distress axis)
- On-device sentence embeddings (TFLite)
- Privacy Shield (noise + normalization + permutation)
- Local database storage (Room)

---

## Product Direction

The long-term vision is not "mood tracking".

It is:

> A personal semantic memory system  
> that reveals patterns in your writing over time

---

## Feature Roadmap

---

# Phase 1 — Foundation Expansion (High Impact, Low Risk)

### 1. Semantic Memory (Highest Priority)
Use embeddings to find related entries.

**Capabilities:**
- Similar entries across time
- “You’ve written about this before” moments
- Memory resurfacing

**Example:**
> “Started a new project today.”

Similar entries:
- March 2025: “Beginning a new startup idea”
- August 2025: “Planning a side project”
- January 2026: “Thinking about building something new”

---

### 2. Semantic Search
Replace keyword search with meaning-based retrieval.

Examples:
- “Times I felt uncertain”
- “Entries about changing direction”
- “Moments of clarity”

No tags required.

---

### 3. Reflection Timeline (Language Trends)
Track how writing evolves over time:

- Entry length trends
- Vocabulary diversity
- Question frequency
- First-person usage (“I”, “me”)
- Future vs past orientation

Focus:

> “How has my writing changed?”

not

> “How do I feel?”

---

### 4. Weekly Reflection Digest (On-device)
Auto-generated summaries:

- Number of entries
- Writing consistency
- Dominant themes (derived from embeddings)
- Structural changes in writing

No emotional labeling.

Pure observation.

---

# Phase 2 — Structure & Meaning Layer

---

### 5. What Have I Been Thinking About?
Cluster entries into themes:

- Career
- Relationships
- Health
- Learning
- Life transitions

No manual tagging required.

Derived purely from embeddings.

---

### 6. Entry Connections Graph
Visualize journal entries as a network:

- Nodes = entries
- Edges = semantic similarity

This allows users to explore how thoughts evolve over time.

---

### 7. Life Chapters (Automatic Clustering)
Detect phases in life:

- New Job Phase
- Travel Period
- Personal Transition
- Learning Phase

Each chapter emerges from clusters of similar embeddings.

---

### 8. Journal Replay
“On this day” but semantic:

Instead of exact dates:
- Show entries similar in meaning from past years

Creates continuity of thought over time.

---

# Phase 3 — Personalization & Expansion

---

### 9. Personal Language Compass (User-Defined Axes)
Allow users to define their own semantic axes.

Examples:

- Confident ↔ Uncertain
- Connected ↔ Isolated
- Focused ↔ Scattered
- Grounded ↔ Restless

Each axis is built from anchor sentences and embedding projection.

Key principle:

> Meaning is defined by the user, not the system.

---

### 10. Multi-Axis Reflection Space ("Star Map")
Instead of numeric dashboards, visualize axes as a constellation:

⭐ Confidence Signal  
⭐ Connection Signal  
⭐ Curiosity Signal  
⭐ Stability Signal  
⭐ Agency Signal  

Each journal entry becomes a point in a semantic universe.

---

## Features to Avoid

### ❌ AI Chat Therapist
- High cost
- Trust issues
- Regulatory risk
- Conflicts with privacy-first philosophy

---

### ❌ Emotion Classification Labels
Avoid outputs like:
- Happy 72%
- Sad 14%
- Angry 10%

These create false precision and psychological framing.

---

### ❌ Mental Health Scoring
Avoid:
- Depression risk
- Anxiety score
- Stress detection

Laniakea is not a diagnostic tool.

---

## Product Philosophy Boundaries

Laniakea should only provide:

- Structural insights (language patterns)
- Temporal changes (how writing evolves)
- Semantic relationships (how thoughts connect)

It should never claim:
- Emotional diagnosis
- Psychological interpretation
- Mental health assessment

---

## What Excites Me Most

The most powerful direction is not scoring emotions.

It is building a **semantic memory system**:

- Entries reconnect across months and years
- Themes naturally emerge without tagging
- Users rediscover forgotten thoughts
- Life becomes a connected narrative rather than isolated notes
